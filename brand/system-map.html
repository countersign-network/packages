One policy in. One freeze out.

Clients hold no keys — they call the Core. The Core compiles a single policy down to each backend's native controls, guards every spend, and on one signal freezes every rail at once. Drive it yourself below.

Flow 01 · the spend guard

Ask before you spend.

Every transaction calls /evaluate first. The Core returns allow, deny, or needs-approval — and the matching native control is already pushed into the rail, so even a direct send that bypasses Countersign is rejected by the backend itself.

01

Within policy → allow

Under the per-tx and rolling daily caps, on the allowlist → the agent gets a green light and the rail enforces the cap natively.

02

Over cap / off-list → deny

Fail-closed. The spend never executes, and action_blocked is written to the ledger with the reason.

03

Above threshold → hold

Routed to a human approval. A freeze always overrides a pending approval.

Flow 02 · the kill switch

Stop everything, fail-closed, under a second.

One signal — from an operator or the anomaly monitor — fans out to every rail concurrently. A backend whose freeze won't confirm is escalated (revoke session), never reported as safe. Every step is countersigned into the ledger.

1 · freeze_requested → written first
2 · freeze() → all rails, concurrent, bounded by timeout
3 · confirmed → freeze_result + freeze_resolved (<1s)
4 · unconfirmed → revokeSession (escalate)
5 · ledger re-verified intact

Proven, not promised

A four-rail live freeze — three crypto wallets and a Visa card — confirmed in ~432ms on testnet, signed hash-chained ledger verified. Run it yourself: pnpm exec tsx packages/agent-harness/live-freeze.ts.
