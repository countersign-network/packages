01 · Brand foundation

What we are, in one line.

The neutral kill switch and audit ledger for AI agents that spend money — one policy, one freeze, one tamper-evident ledger across every wallet vendor at once. Every word of copy ladders up to that sentence.

The name

To countersign is to add a second, approving signature to something already initiated. That is exactly the product: the second signer on an agent's transaction — the pre-flight guard, the human approval, the freeze. Not "co-sign" (jointly); countersign (approve after).

The category

Cross-vendor agent-spend governance. We are a network, not a wallet — hence .network. We never take custody; we hold the policy, the freeze, and the proof.

The proof

A single falsifiable claim anchors the brand: freeze agents across four rails at once, in ~432ms, with a tamper-evident ledger of every attempt. Lead with the number; invite people to falsify it.

Brand attributes

Neutral

Vendor-agnostic by construction. We never favor a rail.

Falsifiable

Claims you can run. Numbers, not adjectives.

Fail-closed

Safety is the default. Doubt means deny.

Legible

Engineered and clear — a blueprint, not a billboard.

Clear space, sizing & don'ts
✓ Do
  • Keep clear space around the mark equal to the height of the check.
  • Render the mark on its dark tile, even over light layouts.
  • Minimum size 20px (favicon) / 24px (UI). Below that, drop the wordmark, keep the mark.
  • Pair the wordmark in Inter Bold at the weight shown.
✕ Don't
  • Recolor the shield or check outside the brand blue / signal green.
  • Add gradients, bevels, or drop shadows to the mark itself.
  • Stretch, rotate, or outline the wordmark.
  • Place the mark on a busy photo without the dark tile behind it.
03 · Color

Near-black canvas, one signal.

The system is monochrome ink with three meaningful hues: brand blue for structure, signal green for the live/safe/allowed state, and used sparingly, amber for caution and red for the freeze. Color carries meaning — never decoration.

05 · Motifs & texture

The blueprint, the aurora, the node.

Three repeatable textures make any surface unmistakably Countersign without a single logo: a hairline blueprint grid, a single signal-green aurora behind the hero, and the + node where structural lines meet.

Blueprint grid

46px lattice at 5% blue, masked toward the corners. The engineering substrate.

Signal aurora

One soft green glow, top-right, behind the hero only. The product is "one signal," visually too.

The + node

A mono plus where a section rule meets the margin, or in a card corner. The blueprint's connection point.

07 · Voice & tone

Precise, falsifiable, calm.

We sound like a security engineer who respects your time: confident because the claim is runnable, never hyped. Lead with the number. Name the threat plainly. Never overstate what's shipped — testnet is testnet.

✓ Write like this
  • "Freeze four rails in ~432ms. Don't take our word for it — run it."
  • "Fail-closed: no decision, no spend."
  • "One policy, compiled to each backend's native controls."
  • "Testnet today; mainnet follows a third-party audit."
✕ Not like this
  • "Revolutionary, AI-powered, next-gen security platform."
  • "Guaranteed to stop all fraud, instantly, forever."
  • Vague superlatives with no number behind them.
  • Implying mainnet/custody we don't yet offer.
Lexicon

Prefer: control plane, rail, kill switch, spend guard, fail-closed, tamper-evident ledger, cross-vendor, neutral. Avoid: "wallet" (we're above wallets), "custody" (we never custody), "blockchain" as a buzzword, "AI" as decoration.
